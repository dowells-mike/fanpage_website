TERMS
COMMONLY USED
IN MANGA

ANIME

a style of Japanese film and television animation, typically aimed at adults as well as children.

EROMANGA

erotic or pornographic manga

HAREM

Harem is a kind of story in Japanese anime and manga where a male character is surrounded and loved by many female characters.

ISEKAI

Isekai is a subgenre of fantasy in which a character is suddenly transported from their world into a new or unfamiliar one.

JOSEI

Josei (女性 meaning woman) is a category of manga marketed to women, typically 18-30 years old.

KAWAII

In Japanese, the word kawaii has a meaning that sits more or less at the juncture of "cute," "tiny," or "lovable."

LIGHT NOVEL

A light novel is a style of Japanese novel targeting teenagers and young adults. These novels contain mostly illustrations in anime or manga style.

LOLITA

The term's meaning and use in Japanese differs substantially from the English, and has developed instead into a positive synonym for the "sweet and adorable" adolescent girl.

MECHA

Mecha is a genre of Japanese manga and anime that heavily features or focuses on mechanical innovation.

OTAKU

a person having an intense or obsessive interest especially in the fields of anime and manga —often used before another noun otaku culture.
